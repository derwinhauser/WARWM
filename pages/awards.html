Honoring Excellence

The World Association for Research on Workplace Mobbing (WARWM) awards individuals for their outstanding contributions to the field of workplace mobbing research to acknowledge and celebrate excellence. These awards recognize achievements such as impactful research, lifetime dedication, and significant contributions to advancing knowledge in workplace mobbing research. By honoring exceptional scholars, WARWM aims to encourage high-quality research, inspire future contributions, and foster a vibrant community dedicated to understanding and shaping workplace mobbing research.

WARWM Awards

Fellow Awards

Recognizing distinguished contributions to the field

Distinguished Service Awards

Honoring exceptional service to WARWM and the research community

Long-term Contribution Awards

Celebrating sustained dedication and impact

Early Career Awards

Supporting emerging scholars in workplace mobbing research

Recognizing outstanding mentorship and guidance

Conference Research Awards

Recognizing outstanding presentations at NCWM

Recognition: Kenneth Westhues

WARWM acknowledges the pioneering work of Kenneth Westhues, whose long-term contributions have been instrumental in establishing and advancing the field of workplace mobbing research. His dedication and scholarship continue to inspire researchers worldwide.
